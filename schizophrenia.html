Schizophrenia

What is Schizophrenia?

Schizophrenia is a chronic and severe mental health disorder that affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves. People with schizophrenia may seem like they have lost touch with reality, which can be distressing for both the individual and their loved ones. (WHO)

Signs and Symptoms

  • Delusions: False beliefs that are not based in reality. (NIMH)
  • Disorganized thinking: Difficulty organizing thoughts or speaking clearly. (NIMH)
  • Withdrawal: Lack of motivation or interest in social activities. (NIMH)

Possible Causes

Schizophrenia is caused by a combination of factors, including:

  • Genetics: A family history of schizophrenia increases the risk. (NIMH)
  • Brain chemistry: Imbalances in neurotransmitters like dopamine. (NIMH)
  • Environment: Stressful life events or trauma. (NIMH)

Treatments and Therapies

Schizophrenia is treatable with a combination of:

  • Medication: Antipsychotic drugs to reduce symptoms. (NIMH)
  • Therapy: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) to manage thoughts and behaviors. (NIMH)
  • Support: Family therapy and community support groups. (NIMH)

Statistics

Here are some key statistics about schizophrenia:

  • Global: 20 million people worldwide. (WHO)
  • Local: 150,000 Australians live with schizophrenia. (SANE Australia)
  • Age: Symptoms typically appear in late teens to early 30s. (NIMH)
  • Gender: Affects men and women equally. (NIMH)

Impact on Life and Relationships

Schizophrenia can affect daily functioning, relationships, and self-esteem. However, with proper treatment and support, individuals can lead fulfilling lives. (NIMH)

Famous People

John Nash: A Nobel Prize winning mathematician who managed his schizophrenia and continued his work. (WebMD)

Eduard Einstein: The youngest child of Albert Einstein who was diagnosed with schizophrenia at age 20. (WebMD)
